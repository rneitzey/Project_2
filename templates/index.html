​
ornadoes, one of Nature’s most violent weather phenomena, hold a special fascination for almost everyone regardless of age. The term “Tornado Magnet” is slang commonly used for mobile homes and mobile home parks. The Department of Homeland Security notes that: “According to the U.S. Census Bureau, 31% of the people that perished in tornadoes between 2009 and 2011 were residing in or fleeing from mobile homes. An inventory of mobile home park locations and the number of mobile homes within those parks is, therefore, essential for emergency preparedness, response, and evacuation.”
Using Tornado track information from the National Center for Environmental Information (NCEI), and mobile home park information from the Department of Homeland Security, students will be able to explore the relationship between tornadoes, as natural hazard phenomenon, and tornado preparedness. Learning objectives include:
  • Seasonal trends
  • Tornado severity/intensity
  • Geography of tornado distributions
  • Severe storm preparedness
